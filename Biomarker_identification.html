Computational Biomarker Discovery for Thyroid Cancer

Using Multi-Omics and Bioinformatics Approaches

üî¨ Project Overview

This research paper explores computational methods for identifying biomarkers in thyroid cancer, utilizing gene expression datasets from the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO). By analyzing differentially expressed genes (DEGs) through tools like EdgeR, DAVID, and KEGG, the study identifies key genes and pathways, such as APOC1, CHI3L1 and SERPINA1, that are crucial in thyroid cancer progression. These findings are pivotal for improving diagnostic precision and developing targeted therapies, demonstrating the power of integrating bioinformatics with clinical research to tackle complex diseases.

üõ† Technologies & Tools

  • Languages: R, Python, Excel
  • Bioinformatics Tools: GEO2R, EdgeR, DAVID, KEGG, STRING, Cytoscape, GSEA
  • Visualization: Heatmaps, PCA, UpSet Plots, PPI Networks
  • Statistical Methods: T-Test, FDR adjustment, Log2FC

üìä Methodology

  1. Data collection from GEO (5 datasets).
  2. Preprocessing and normalization in R and Excel.
  3. Differential gene expression analysis using EdgeR and T-Test.
  4. Integration of DEGs across datasets.
  5. Functional annotation via DAVID and KEGG.
  6. Protein interaction mapping with STRING and Cytoscape.
  7. Survival analysis with GSEA.

üß¨ Key Findings

  • Top Biomarkers: APOE, SERPINA1, CHI3L1, APOC1, HMGA2, LRP1B, LIPH.
  • Pathways Involved: Notch signaling, TGF-beta, cholesterol metabolism, immune response.
  • Biological Insight: Strong association of lipid metabolism and immune evasion in thyroid cancer.
